Key sections
Contact Information: Full name, phone number, email, and a relevant LinkedIn profile URL.
Professional Summary: A brief, impactful introduction that highlights your years of experience, key skills, and major achievements, using keywords from the job description.
Skills: A list of both hard skills (e.g., Salesforce CRM, data analysis, negotiation) and soft skills (e.g., persuasion, time management, communication) that are relevant to the job.
Work Experience:
List jobs in reverse chronological order.
For each role, include your job title, company name, location, and dates of employment.
Use bullet points to describe responsibilities and achievements, focusing on quantifiable results rather than just duties.
Use action verbs and include metrics such as revenue generated, quotas exceeded (e.g., "exceeded sales targets by 30% YoY"), or new clients acquired.
Education: Include your degree, university, and graduation date. Relevant coursework or certifications can also be listed.
Certifications: List any sales-related certifications, such as "Certification in Sales Management" from a known institution. 
General tips
Keep it concise: Aim for one page, and use a maximum of two pages for extensive experience.
Use a clean format: Opt for a professional, easy-to-read font and layout with adequate white space.
Quantify your achievements: Use numbers and metrics to prove your impact and make your accomplishments tangible.
Tailor to the job: Read job descriptions carefully to identify and incorporate relevant keywords and skills.
Be specific: Avoid generic statements and provide specific examples of your success, such as being named top sales associate for a specific period

Make your contact information prominent
Display your contact information prominently so employers can find it easily. You can include your name, mailing address and phone number. While including your email address, make sure it sounds professional. You can also create a separate email account for work-related correspondence to prevent important emails from getting mixed up in your regular inbox.
You can also include a web page address if you have one. Avoid linking to blogs or social media pages if they are unrelated to your career.

Write a professional summary
Your resume summary should include one or two sentences highlighting your key achievements and skills.
Examples:
Award-winning, result-driven sales executive with more than 15 years of experience in showroom sales.
Known for consistent sales achievement, including a 95% customer satisfaction rating and 97% customer retention.

List your professional skills

List your education and certifications
Include degree, university, location, and relevant achievements. Add certifications with issuing body, date of issue, and expiration date if applicable.

List your professional affiliations
Mention memberships in relevant organisations and leadership roles, if any.

Tips for creating a good resume:
- Proofread carefully.
- Align skills with each employer's needs.
- Keep to 1–2 pages.
- Use a simple, professional format.
- Review resumes by others in your field.
- Highlight measurable achievements.

A well-structured Sales Executive resume is about strategically organizing your profile to highlight your qualifications, key competencies, and accomplishments in a format that’s visually appealing and ATS-friendly. Recruiters spend only 6-8 seconds on an initial scan of your resume, which means the structure must guide their eyes toward the most critical information fast.

Now, follow this proven structure to create a winning resume that aligns with what hiring managers are looking for.

1. Header
This is your resume’s calling card. Keep it clean, minimal, and professional. Avoid unnecessary graphics, and ensure the contact details are up-to-date.

Include:
Full name
Professional title
Phone number
Email address
LinkedIn profile URL

2. Professional Summary
Your Sales Executive resume summary should be a 2–3-line elevator pitch highlighting your core strengths, major achievements, and what you bring to the role.

Example:
Proficient Sales Executive with over 4 years of experience in B2B enterprise sales, known for exceeding sales targets by up to 30% YoY. Skilled in lead generation, CRM, and consultative selling.

3. Skills Section
Include a mix of hard skills and soft skills.

4. Professional Experience
Focus on quantifiable achievements.
Use STAR method.
3–5 bullet points per job.

5. Education
Keep this section clean and simple.

6. Certifications
Include industry-recognized certifications.

7. Additional Sections (Optional)
Awards, languages, volunteer work, projects.
