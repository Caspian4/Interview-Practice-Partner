Hard skills
These are technical, measurable, and often learned through training: 
CRM software proficiency: Using tools like Salesforce to manage customer relationships.
Product knowledge: Having an in-depth understanding of the products or services being sold.
Data analysis: Analyzing sales data to find trends and opportunities.
Sales technology: Proficiency with productivity apps, sales enablement tools, and social media selling.
Sales techniques: Knowledge of specific methods, like cold calling, prospecting, and closing strategies.
Industry knowledge: Understanding the market, competitors, and sales process. 
Soft skills
These are interpersonal and behavioral traits that help with interactions and relationships: 
Communication: Clearly and effectively conveying information to clients.
Active listening: Paying close attention to what clients are saying to understand their needs.
Empathy: Understanding and sharing the feelings of another person.
Negotiation: Reaching mutually beneficial agreements.
Resilience: Remaining positive and persistent in the face of rejection and tough conditions.
Adaptability: Adjusting to different situations and client needs.
Relationship building: Developing and maintaining long-term relationships with clients.
Problem-solving: Finding solutions to customer issues and objections.
Time management: Prioritizing tasks to maximize productivity.

Communication Skills
Clear and persuasive communication is the cornerstone of every successful sales interaction. Sales executives must be able to simplify complex ideas, ask the right questions, and build trust with diverse audiences.

According to LinkedIn’s State of Sales report, 89% of buyers describe the salespeople they ultimately do business with as “trusted advisors,” not pushy closers.

Persuasion and Negotiation
A skilled sales executive knows how to handle objections, ease concerns, and influence decisions without sounding forceful. They balance assertiveness with empathy to guide prospects toward conversion. Techniques like SPIN selling or the “Feel-Felt-Found” method are proven strategies used by high-converting sales reps across industries.

Deep Product Knowledge
Understanding the features, benefits, and ROI of the product or service is non-negotiable. A sales executive should position the offering as a solution—not just a transaction.

For Example: In tech or SaaS sales, this might mean explaining integrations, security protocols, and long-term value in business terms.

CRM and Digital Savviness
Modern sales professionals must be proficient with CRM platforms like Salesforce, HubSpot, or Zoho. Knowing how to track leads, automate tasks, and interpret dashboards is crucial for staying organized and data-driven.

Why it’s essential: McKinsey research shows that companies using advanced CRM analytics experience a 15–20% boost in sales productivity.

Time and Task Management
Sales executives juggle multiple responsibilities—from prospecting and presentations to reporting and follow-ups. Prioritizing high-impact activities is what separates top performers from average ones.

Educational Requirements
Bachelor’s Degree in Business Administration, Marketing, Commerce, or a related field is usually the minimum requirement.
An MBA or postgraduate diploma in Sales or Marketing is a plus and often leads to quicker promotion into managerial roles.
Professional Certifications (Optional but Valuable)
Certifications show commitment to continuous learning and add credibility during interviews or internal promotions. Some widely recognized options include:

Sandler Sales Training
SPIN Selling Certification
HubSpot Inbound Sales Certification
Google Digital Sales Certification
Many companies now use certifications as a benchmark when shortlisting sales candidates—especially in competitive sectors like IT, finance, and FMCG.

Work Experience
1–3 years of experience in sales, customer service, telesales, or field sales is preferred for entry-to-mid-level roles.
Candidates with prior experience in B2B sales, retail, or SaaS have an edge due to industry-specific know-how.
